Struggling through short repeated runs at high bodyweight is a load problem, not an engine problem. Recorded VO2 max sits solidly average for age and gender — the aerobic base is there. The ceiling isn't fitness. It's bodyweight. Meaningful weight loss produces measurable performance gains in every session, every run, and every race station without changing a single thing about the training.
Hyrox is structured as 8 × 1km runs, each followed by a functional station. The running is where the race is decided — stations are fixed and predictable, running pace is where time is gained or lost. The training block is designed to build an aerobic engine capable of sustaining pace across all eight runs, even after the station work.
Strengths Coming In
  • Prior weight loss trajectory demonstrates the capacity for sustained multi-month adherence
  • CrossFit background: rowing, wall balls, carries, and conditioning are not starting from zero
  • Strong lean mass base — strength-to-bodyweight ratio improves meaningfully as the scale drops
  • Recorded VO2 max and resting HR suggest the cardiovascular system is more capable than race-day feel suggests
  • Tracking infrastructure in place: Whoop, MFP, Apple Watch — no setup lag
What This Block Develops
  • Running under station fatigue — the specific Hyrox adaptation. Running well after station work is untrained. Phase 2 addresses this directly.
  • 90-minute sustained output — CrossFit is interval-based. Hyrox is continuous elevated HR for ~90 minutes. The Zone 2 block builds the specific demand.
  • Station mechanics — SkiErg, sled, sandbag lunges require deliberate practice. CrossFit doesn't cover them.
  • Race pacing — going too hard on runs 1–3 degrades every station from 5 onward. Needs specific training to lock in.

Phase Structure
Throughout: 3 weeks on, 1 week deload. Deload weeks reduce CrossFit to 3 sessions, run volume to 70%, and Zone 2 add-ons to 20 minutes. The load comes down — training doesn't stop. Deload weeks: Week 4, Week 8, Week 12.
Phase 1 · Weeks 1–9 · 29 Mar – 26 May
Base Build — Murph Block

Murph programming runs until 26 May (Memorial Day weekend). The block is endurance-biased — that's exactly the right stimulus for building an aerobic base ahead of Hyrox. One session per week carries a Murph-style structure with heavier running load. On those weeks, total run volume for the day (CF + Runna) is elevated — manage cumulative fatigue accordingly.

  • CrossFit: 4x/week following your box's programming.
  • Running: Runna 5km rebuild until 26 April. Switch to Runna Hyrox plan on 27 April — input 2 July as race date. Continue 2x/week throughout Phase 1.
  • Zone 2 add-ons: Stack 30–40min Stairmaster or static bike onto both Runna sessions immediately after. Target HR 141–151 bpm. Must stay conversational. Deload weeks: 20min only.
  • No station-specific work yet. Building the base before layering station practice on top of it.
  • Nutrition: 2,350 kcal / 210P / 215C / 72F from Day 1.
  • Deload weeks: Week 4 (21 Apr) and Week 8 (19 May).
  • CF In-House Comp (Sat 3 May): Full effort. No taper. Training day with a crowd.
  • Target: On pace to lose ~40% of your total goal by 26 May
Transition · 26–27 April
Switch Runna Plans

5km rebuild ends 26 April. On 27 April: open Runna, start the Hyrox plan, set race date to 2 July. Answer the fitness setup questions accurately — the plan builds backwards from the race date at your current level. ~9 weeks to race day at this point.

Phase 2 · Weeks 10–13 · 27 May – 22 Jun
Hyrox-Specific Build

CrossFit shifts back to strength and mixed-modal post-Murph. Running stays on the Runna Hyrox plan. Station-specific work is added as deliberate accessory within CF — not full race simulations, but targeted movement practice and muscular endurance.

  • CrossFit: 4x/week. Weighted lunges in every session with accessory time. Sandbag lunges and burpee broad jumps are the two highest-risk stations — they need volume from this point forward.
  • Running: Runna Hyrox plan continues. Zone 2 add-ons continue post-run.
  • Zone 2 rotation: Introduce SkiErg and C2 rowing into Zone 2 add-ons alongside Stairmaster/bike. Builds Hyrox-specific muscular endurance on race equipment at aerobic intensity. 20–40min at 141–151 bpm.
  • Backwards sled pull: 3–4 sets as CF accessory, 1–2x/week. Light load. Walk backwards pulling the sled — knee and IT band mobility work. Controlled, not heavy.
  • Sled push: Find a gym with a sled before 27 May. 3–4 practice sessions before race day — mechanics need deliberate learning before you race them.
  • Deload: Week 12 (16 Jun). 3x CF, maintain running, shorten Zone 2.
  • Nutrition: Review at Week 10. If within 1kg of 100kg target, shift to maintenance (~2,750 kcal, ~280g carbs) to support performance into race week.
  • Target: ~90% of total goal loss achieved by 22 Jun
Race Week · 23 Jun – 2 Jul
Taper

The fitness built over 13 weeks is set. This week is about arriving fresh. Nothing added here improves race-day performance — the risk is damage, not gain.

  • Mon 23 Jun: Light CF — skill work only, no conditioning. 30min max.
  • Tue 24 Jun: Easy run 3–4km, conversational. No Zone 2 add-on.
  • Wed 25 Jun: Full rest or 20min walk.
  • Thu 26 Jun: Activation only — 15min SkiErg or row, moderate pace.
  • Fri 27 Jun: Full rest. Eat well. Sleep 8hrs. Sodium loading starts.
  • Sat 28 Jun: Rest or travel. Light walk only.
  • Sun 29 Jun–Wed 1 Jul: Rest. Eat carbs. Sleep.
  • Thu 2 July: Race Day

Maintenance Baseline (3,300 kcal TDEE)
3,300 kcal

Estimated TDEE at starting bodyweight with CrossFit 4x/week + 2x running. This is the maintenance level — everything below creates a deficit, everything above gains.

Fat Loss Target — Use This Daily
2,350 kcal
Protein210g · 840 kcal
Carbs215g · 860 kcal
Fat72g · 648 kcal

950 kcal/day below maintenance. At this training volume: ~0.8–0.9kg fat loss/week. Sufficient for 10kg loss over 14 weeks with muscle retention at 210g protein.

Race prep (Weeks 12–14): If within 1kg of 100kg target, shift to maintenance. Bring carbs to ~280g. Performance is the priority for the final two weeks — arriving at the start line glycogen-depleted is not a strategy.
DecisionReasoning
Why 2,350 not higher?950 kcal/day below a 3,300 TDEE. Produces ~0.8–0.9kg/week fat loss. Sufficient to hit 100kg by July 2 with a small buffer. Higher calories slow progress; lower calories increase muscle loss risk at this training load.
Why 210g protein?~1.9g/kg lean mass — upper end of evidence-based targets for muscle preservation in aggressive deficit + high training volume. Protein also has the highest thermic effect of any macro — processing it costs calories.
Why not cut fat lower?Fat floor at 65g preserves testosterone production and cortisol regulation during deficit. Going below increases hormonal disruption risk without meaningful additional fat loss.
The adjustment dialAlways carbs first. Protein stays at 210g. Fat stays above 65g. Carbs are the only lever. Up 150 kcal if losing too fast. Down 150 kcal if stalling after MFP audit.

Aerobic Zone Calculator
What this is: A pace and intensity calculator that takes two simple timed tests — a 1-minute max effort and a 6-minute max effort — and outputs your personalised training zones for running, rowing, and SkiErg. These zones calibrate the intensity of your Zone 2 add-on sessions and erg work in Phase 2, so you're training at the right level rather than guessing.
How to run the tests — do these on separate days or with 30+ minutes rest between them:
1-min test: Max effort for exactly 60 seconds on treadmill/track or erg. Record metres covered.
6-min test: Max sustained effort for exactly 6 minutes. Record metres covered. This is your primary test — give it full effort. It's the basis for all zone calculations.
When: Run these once at the start of Phase 2 (late May) and again at Week 12 to see how zones have shifted. Don't test during deload weeks or when fatigued.

Supplement Stack
Hierarchy: calories → protein → sleep → training → then supplements. Tier 1 is non-negotiable at this training load and deficit depth. Tier 2 if budget allows. Tier 3 optional. Don't spend on anything not listed here.
TIER 1 — NON-NEGOTIABLE
Creatine Monohydrate
5g/day · Post-workout · No loading phase needed

Most well-evidenced supplement in sports science. Supports phosphocreatine resynthesis for repeated high-intensity efforts — direct relevance to CrossFit and Hyrox station work. Supports lean mass retention during caloric deficit. Already in use — keep it.

Evidence: Extremely Strong
Whey Protein
As needed to hit 210g/day · Post-workout

A food supplement, not a magic solution. Use it to close the gap to 210g daily. Post-workout timing is optimal — MPS peaks 1–2hrs after training. Hit 210g on rest days too: muscle synthesis happens during recovery, not during the session.

Evidence: Extremely Strong
Vitamin D3 + K2
2,000–4,000 IU D3 + 100mcg K2 · Daily with fat-containing meal

High training load combined with caloric deficit increases demand. D3 supports testosterone production, immune function, and bone density. K2 directs calcium to bone rather than arteries. Take together. Blood test is the most accurate way to determine precise dosing.

Evidence: Strong
Omega-3 (Fish Oil)
2–3g EPA+DHA combined/day · With meals

Anti-inflammatory, supports muscle protein synthesis, improves recovery quality. Elevated systemic inflammation in a caloric deficit with high training load is expected — omega-3s buffer this. Verify the label shows EPA + DHA separately: 2–3g of those specifically, not just 2–3g total fish oil.

Evidence: Strong
TIER 2 — RECOMMENDED
Caffeine
3–5mg/kg · 45–60min pre-training · 1–2 caffeine-free days/week

At 110kg: ~330–550mg. Start at the lower end. The critical practice: maintain 1–2 caffeine-free days per week so race-day dosing produces a full acute response. Use caffeine-free days on lower-stakes sessions. Avoid on rest days.

Evidence: Extremely Strong
Magnesium Glycinate
300–400mg elemental · Before bed

High training volume plus caloric deficit depletes magnesium. Deficiency directly impairs sleep quality, muscle recovery, and energy metabolism. Glycinate is best absorbed with minimal GI effects — oxide form is largely ineffective. Night-time dosing supports sleep quality, which is a direct performance input.

Evidence: Moderate–Strong
Beta-Alanine
3.2–6.4g/day · Split doses · Start now — 4 weeks to load

Raises muscle carnosine, which buffers lactic acid during sustained high-intensity efforts. Directly relevant to 90 minutes of Hyrox stations. The tingling (paraesthesia) is harmless — split dosing reduces it. Start immediately if using: tissue loading takes 4+ weeks. Starting at Week 10 is too late for race day benefit.

Evidence: Moderate–Strong
Electrolytes
Sessions over 60min + race day · Sodium focus

Sodium and potassium replacement matters for performance and recovery in a deficit with high sweat output. Add an electrolyte tab to longer CF sessions and all erg work. Race day: sodium load the night before and morning of. Precision Hydration PH1000 or equivalent.

Evidence: Moderate
TIER 3 — OPTIONAL
Citrulline Malate
6–8g · 45–60min pre-training

Supports nitric oxide production and ammonia clearance during high-rep training. Consistent moderate evidence for reduced perceived fatigue in longer sessions. A useful addition to the pre-workout stack for longer CF sessions and erg work from Phase 2.

Evidence: Moderate
Collagen + Vitamin C
10–15g collagen + 50mg Vit C · 30–60min pre-training

Emerging evidence for tendon and connective tissue synthesis when timed before loading. At your bodyweight, running and weighted lunges create significant connective tissue load. Low risk. Potentially meaningful for injury prevention as running volume increases through Phase 1 and 2.

Evidence: Emerging

Race Day — Thursday 2 July 2026
The race is decided by 13 weeks of training, not the 24 hours before. Race day execution is about protecting what's already been built — pacing conservatively in the early runs so the later stations and runs don't collapse. Start slow, finish strong.
Morning Protocol
  • Wake 2.5–3 hours before wave start
  • Familiar carb-rich breakfast — same food you've eaten before training. Nothing new on race day.
  • 200–300mg caffeine 45–60min before start (only if trained with it throughout the block)
  • Sodium pre-load: electrolyte drink with breakfast, not just water
  • 2 gels in your pocket for on-course use. Don't rely solely on provided nutrition.
  • No new shoes. No new kit. Nothing untested.
Race Execution
  • Runs 1–4: Conversational pace. ~6:30–7:00/km. If you can't finish a sentence, slow down.
  • Every station: Breathe before starting. Settle into rhythm. Don't sprint the opening reps.
  • Run 4 (halfway): Take a gel. Keep moving while you eat it.
  • Runs 5–7: Legs will feel heavy — this is expected and normal. Shorten stride, increase cadence.
  • Sandbag lunges: Breathe at the turnaround. Keep moving forward — stopping makes restarting harder.
  • Wall balls: Rhythm over speed. Full squat depth — judges no-rep short squats. The finish line is right there.
  • Run 8: Give what's left. This is what the 14 weeks were for.
